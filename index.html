Great intro, but who am I ?

Before I turned 13, I had never even touched a computer. But when I finally did, it was love at first sight. My curiosity was insatiableâ€”I needed to understand how everything worked. This burning desire led me to delve into programming. I started with C++, creating my first simple programs, and then transitioned to Python. Each new language and concept I learned only deepened my passion for coding.

As I ventured further into programming, I became intrigued by web development. The complexity and creativity involved fascinated me. During my exploration, I stumbled upon articles about web hacking. This new world captured my imagination completely. I decided to immerse myself in ethical hacking, publishing scripts, and participating in Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions. These experiences were pivotal, enhancing my problem-solving skills and expanding my understanding of cybersecurity.

As I continued to grow as a programmer, I became curious about how operating systems worked. The idea of diving deep into the core of a computer's functionality intrigued me, so I began exploring OS development. This journey into the low-level workings of computers was challenging but incredibly rewarding. It taught me the importance of efficiency, memory management, and the intricate relationship between hardware and software. Through building and experimenting with my own small operating systems, I developed a deeper understanding of the foundations of computing.

Now, as a 17-year-old self-taught programmer, I look back on my journey with pride. From a curious beginner with no computer experience to a developer with a diverse skill set and real-world experience, my path has been both challenging and incredibly fulfilling. The journey is far from over, but the lessons learned and the milestones achieved have set a strong foundation for my future in tech.
